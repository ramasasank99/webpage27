Spix's Macaw

Spix's Macaw currently exists in captivity, with numbers ranging from a frighteningly low 60 to 80. This bird is also known as the "Little Blue Macaw" because It is known for its brilliant blue plumage.centuries of deforestation and habitat degradation, then capture for the bird trade, brought an end to the wild population.

Pink-Headed Duck

The Pink-headed Duck was always considered rare, but it has not been conclusively seen in the wild since 1949 in India and is known from Myanmar from only two individuals.

Indian Asiatic Cheetah

Cheetahs are the fastest animals on Earth, and Asiatic cheetahs are no exception. Their incredible speed enables them to hunt even the fastest of prey, like gazelle, wild sheep, hare, and wild goat. Unfortunately, it is highly unlikely that these magnificent creatures will ever be seen in India again.
